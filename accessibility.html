Accessibility Statement

Everyone is welcome here

Last Updated: March 2025

Christ and Coffee LLC is committed to ensuring that our website and community are accessible and welcoming to everyone — regardless of ability, background, or circumstance. Just as every woman has a seat at our table, every visitor deserves full access to our digital home.

Our Commitment

We are actively working to ensure that christandcoffee.net meets or exceeds the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards. These guidelines help make web content more accessible to people with disabilities including visual, auditory, physical, speech, cognitive, and neurological disabilities.

What We Are Doing

To improve accessibility across our website we are committed to the following:

  • Clear visual design — high contrast colors, readable fonts and clean layouts
  • Descriptive image alt text — all images include descriptions for screen readers
  • Keyboard navigation — our site can be navigated without a mouse
  • Readable text — font sizes and spacing designed for easy reading
  • Simple language — content written in plain, clear English
  • Mobile friendly — our site works on all screen sizes and devices
  • No flashing content — we avoid content that may trigger seizures

In-Person Accessibility

Christ and Coffee is committed to being physically accessible as well. When planning events and meetups we prioritize venues that are:

  • Wheelchair accessible
  • Easy to find and navigate
  • Comfortable and inclusive for all women

If you have specific accessibility needs for any of our in-person events please reach out to us in advance at [email protected] and we will do our best to accommodate you.

Known Limitations

While we strive for full accessibility we acknowledge that some areas of our website may still be improving. We are a small ministry team doing our best and we are committed to continual improvement. If you encounter any barriers please let us know.

Our Heart

At Christ and Coffee we believe that every woman — regardless of ability, background, denomination or season of life — deserves to feel welcomed, valued and included. Accessibility is not just a legal requirement for us — it is an extension of our core value of community. You belong here.
